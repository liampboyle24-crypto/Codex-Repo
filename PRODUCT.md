# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Director-level and C-suite stakeholders responsible for workplace technology, IT operations, facilities, finance, or enterprise collaboration environments. They are evaluating whether to onboard a service provider for AV and collaboration environments and need to understand the business risk, operational cost, accountability model, and credibility of IMS before agreeing to a discussion.

## Product Purpose

This surface should make unreliable AV and collaboration environments feel like a leadership-level business problem, not a room-by-room technical annoyance. It must show the hidden costs of unreliable meeting technology, make the pain points tangible, explain IMS's managed-services approach to total environmental accountability, introduce who IMS is, differentiate IMS from reactive AV vendors and staffing-only providers, and drive a clear CTA to engage IMS for a consultative discussion.

## Source Material

Use https://managed-services-hosted-site.pages.dev/ as the raw language and factual source for IMS managed services messaging, proof points, service pillars, contact details, and the IMS logo reference. Do not reuse that site's visual design system, layout logic, section styling, color palette, typography, effects, or overall design DNA.

Key source messages to preserve or reinterpret:

- Reliable collaboration starts before the meeting does.
- Install day is not the finish line; long-term room reliability, visibility, support, and refresh planning are the business problem.
- AV downtime creates hidden costs in executive time, IT distraction, lost trust, and surprise budget events.
- IMS positions managed services as an operating model: monitor, detect, resolve, report, recommend, and refresh.
- IMS differentiates itself through continuous monitoring, defined ownership, lifecycle planning, leadership-ready reporting, 4Sight, staff augmentation, AVaaS, cybersecurity discipline, and one accountable owner.
- Proof points include 2025 Integrator of the Year, ISO/IEC 27001:2022 certification, 30+ years of AV experience, 5x consecutive Commercial Integrator awards, Inc. 5000 recognition, and Best Place to Work recognition.
- CTA should invite a low-friction discussion about where the prospect's AV stands today and what recommendation fits their environment.

## Brand Personality

Executive, accountable, operational, and quietly confident. The voice should feel like a senior operator explaining business risk with clarity and restraint: direct enough to create urgency, credible enough for leadership, and specific enough to avoid marketing fluff.

## Anti-references

Do not make this feel like an AI-generated generic landing page. Avoid copied design DNA from the current hosted source site, generic SaaS hero layouts, predictable card grids, decorative gradient text, stock-tech abstraction, vague “modern workplace” imagery, tiny uppercase eyebrows on every section, and unsupported claims. Avoid making IMS sound like a commodity AV installer, break/fix support desk, or staffing agency.

## Design Principles

1. **Lead with business consequence**: frame unreliable AV as hidden executive cost, IT drag, reputational risk, and budget unpredictability.
2. **Make accountability concrete**: every section should clarify who owns the environment, what IMS sees, what IMS does, and what leadership receives.
3. **Show the operating model**: communicate managed services as a closed loop, not a menu of disconnected offerings.
4. **Earn executive trust quickly**: use proof points, certification, experience, and named outcomes with restraint and specificity.
5. **Break category reflexes**: use a fresh communication structure and visual approach that shares no design DNA with the current source site.
6. **Drive a consultative next step**: CTA should feel like an expert environment assessment, not a generic sales form.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA at minimum. The page should be readable for executive audiences scanning quickly on desktop, tablet, and mobile; preserve strong contrast, visible focus states, keyboard navigability, reduced-motion alternatives, and clear non-color cues for comparisons, proof, and service categories.
